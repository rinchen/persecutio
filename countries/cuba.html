Cuba

Historical Background

Catholicism has dominated Cuba since colonial times; the 19th-century wars of independence were shaped by tensions between Spanish Catholic authority and Creole identity. After 1959, Fidel Castro’s government confiscated church property, expelled clergy, and banned Christian education. The Soviet period saw atheistic state ideology enforced almost absolutely; reforms began in the late 1980s and 1990s.

Modern-Day Situation

Cuba has experienced improved but uneven religious freedom since the 1990s. The Catholic Church has gained capacity for public social service, yet restrictions persist on registration of new churches, printing of religious materials, and access to state media. Leaders of independent Protestant house churches sometimes report harassment.

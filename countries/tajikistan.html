Historical Background

Tajikistan's religious landscape reflects Soviet-era atheism and traditional Sunni Islam. Small Orthodox and Protestant communities exist, but post-independence laws have tightened religious control.

Sources: (2024) Open Doors World Watch List 2024

Modern-Day Situation

The government restricts all religious activity outside state-approved bodies. Unregistered churches face fines and closure. Children under 18 are banned from religious services, and religious education is heavily regulated.

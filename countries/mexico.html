Historical Background

Catholicism shaped colonial New Spain for three centuries. After independence, Mexico enacted restrictive constitutions and fought the Cristero War (1926-1929), which killed thousands. Religious freedom was restored through late-20th-century legal reforms.

Sources: (2024) Open Doors World Watch List 2024

Modern-Day Situation

Organized crime and local power disputes drive much anti-Christian violence. Priests have been kidnapped and killed, churches burned or extorted, and indigenous Catholic communities displaced in Chiapas, Michoacan, and Guerrero. Clergy defending migrants are also at risk. The increased presence of several criminal groups and their struggle for territorial control has caused Christians and church leaders living in affected areas to face the constant risk of being targeted. This occurs whenever Christians are perceived as being a threat to criminal operations or have disregarded criminal group demands. The constitution provides all persons the right to religious freedom, including the right to engage in religious ceremonies and acts of worship. The constitution declares the country a secular state.

Mexico

Historical

Catholicism arrived with the conquest in 1519; evangelization was tied to colonial governance. After independence (1821), Mexico oscillated between anti-clerical constitutions (1857, 1917) and periods of church-state conflict including the Cristero War (1926–1929). Religious freedom was gradually restored from the late 20th century onward.

Modern

Violence against clergy and church property in Mexico is primarily linked to organized crime and local disputes over land and influence. In regions such as Chihuahua, Michoacán, Guerrero, and Oaxaca, priests have been kidnapped and killed, and churches extorted or burned. Indigenous Catholic communities in Chiapas have faced displacement.

Nigeria

Historical Background

Christianity arrived in Nigeria in the 15th century via Portuguese explorers and missionaries, later deepening through 19th-century missionary activity. The religious violence of recent decades has roots in colonial legacies, post-independence political tensions, the 1967–1970 Biafran War, and the spread of Salafi-jihadist ideology across the Sahel in the late 20th century.

Modern-Day Situation

Nigeria faces overlapping violence from Boko Haram, ISWAP, and armed bandits in the north, and communal violence in the Middle Belt. Christian villagers, pastors, and schools have been targeted in attacks involving killings, kidnappings, and displacement. Hundreds of churches have been destroyed or closed. Intercommunal violence along farmer–herder lines often carries religious overtones in states near the Jos Plateau.

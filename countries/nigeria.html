Historical Background

Christianity arrived through Portuguese traders in the 15th century and expanded through 19th-century missions and colonial education. Post-independence political tensions, the 1967-1970 Biafran War, and the late-20th-century spread of Salafi-jihadist ideology in the Sahel created fertile ground for present-day violence.

Sources: (2024) Open Doors World Watch List 2024

Modern-Day Situation

Boko Haram, ISWAP, and criminal bandits have killed, kidnapped, and displaced Christian villagers and pastors across northern Nigeria. Hundreds of churches have been destroyed. In the Middle Belt, communal violence often carries religious dimensions along farmer-herder lines near the Jos Plateau.

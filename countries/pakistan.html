Historical Background

Catholicism arrived with Portuguese traders; Protestant missions expanded under British rule in Punjab and Sindh. Partition created a Muslim-majority state with large Christian minorities centered in Punjab. Anti-blasphemy legislation hardened under Zia-ul-Haq in the 1980s, and since then legal and societal pressures have increased.

Sources: (2024) Open Doors World Watch List 2024

Modern-Day Situation

Blasphemy laws can carry life imprisonment or death and have been used against Christians. Forced conversions and marriages of Christian girls remain common. Churches are occasionally attacked during holidays or communal tensions, and security for worship remains weak. Pakistan – Persecution Dynamics – December 2024 The constitution establishes Islam as the state religion and requires all provisions of the law to be consistent with Islam. The constitution states, “Subject to law, public order, and morality, every citizen shall have the right to profess, practice, and propagate his religion.” The penal code sets punishments for blasphemy that range from 10 years in prison to the death… Authorities’ failure to address mob violence associated with blasphemy accusations, as well as the forced conversions to Islam of individuals from religious minorities—including Christians, Hindus, and Sikhs—continues to reinforce a climate of fear and religious discrimination.

Pakistan

Historical Background

Catholics arrived with Portuguese traders and missionaries in the 16th century; Protestant missions flourished under British rule in Punjab and Sindh. Partition in 1947 created a Muslim-majority state with significant Christian minorities, particularly in Punjab. Anti-blasphemy legislation hardened from the 1980s onward under General Zia-ul-Haq.

Modern-Day Situation

Pakistan’s blasphemy laws carry severe penalties, including death, and are frequently invoked against Christians. In 2022, Aanita, a Christian woman, and her family faced renewed threats after acquittal. Forced conversions and marriages of Christian girls remain a chronic issue. Churches are sometimes attacked during holidays, and security at places of worship remains a statewide concern.

Historical Background

Catholicism dominated Brazil after Portuguese colonization, and evangelical Protestantism expanded rapidly in the 20th century. Religious competition sometimes triggers local tension, especially among indigenous communities and in favelas, but these have not reached systematic persecution.

Sources: (2024) Open Doors World Watch List 2024

Modern-Day Situation

Brazil guarantees religious freedom and has one of the world's largest Christian populations. Occasional incidents of vandalism or anti-Christian rhetoric occur, but large-scale or state-led persecution is not present.

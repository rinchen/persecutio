Somalia

Historical

Early Christian communities existed on the Somali coast through trade with the Byzantine and Ethiopian worlds. From the 19th century onward, European Catholics and Anglicans established mission stations; most expelled or closed during Siad Barre’s socialist rule after 1969. Civil war destroyed remaining structures in the 1990s.

Modern

Somalia’s federal government has no capacity to protect religious minorities. Al-Shabaab executes individuals known or suspected of converting to Christianity. Any Christian witness is forced underground; foreign workers suspected of religious activity face expulsion.

Saudi Arabia

Historical

Christianity has deep historical roots in Arabia via Nabataean and Ghassanid communities, but became extinct on most of the Arabian Peninsula after the rise of Islam in the 7th century. From the 18th century onward, Catholic and Protestant missionaries operated under Ottoman and Hashemite authority; expansion came after oil-driven contact with the West in the 20th century.

Modern

Saudi Arabia’s legal system is based on an interpretation of Sharia law under which conversion from Islam is legally punishable and can carry the death penalty. Though private worship by expatriate Christians sometimes occurs discreetly, organized Christian fellowship and public displays of faith are prohibited. Bibles are illegal to import and distribute.

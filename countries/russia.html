Historical Background

Russian Orthodoxy dominated for centuries. Soviet atheism suppressed all religion; post-Soviet revival favored Orthodoxy. Recent laws restricting proselytization and foreign religious ties have increased pressure on minority faiths.

Sources: (2024) Open Doors World Watch List 2024

Modern-Day Situation

Anti-evangelism laws restrict religious activity outside registered sites. Protestant and Catholic communities face raids, fines, and denial of registration. Jehovah's Witnesses are banned, and foreign religious workers are expelled. The constitution declares the state is secular and guarantees freedom of religion, equal rights irrespective of religious belief, and the right to worship and profess one’s religion. From archived reports

USCIRF finding: Religious freedom conditions in Russia are particularly severe as the government criminalizes the activities of multiple peaceful religious groups, including Jehovah’s Witnesses, often labeling such organizations as “terrorist,” “extremist,” or “undesirable.” Russia frequently persecutes religious leaders for expressing religiously-based opposition to the Russian invasion of Ukraine and enforces its legal framework on the religious activity on the Ukrainian territory it illegally occupies.

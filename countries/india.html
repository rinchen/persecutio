India

Historical Background

Thomas the Apostle is traditionally believed to have evangelized the Malabar Coast in the 1st century. After Portuguese arrival in 1498, Catholic missions grew; Protestant missions expanded from the 18th century. Anti- Christian legislation in colonial and princely states recurred, as did communal violence around the time of Partition and in later decades.

Modern-Day Situation

India, while religiously plural, has witnessed a rise in violence and intimidation targeting Christians, particularly following the 2014 election of the BJP-led central government. “Anti-conversion” laws enacted in several states restrict religious change and have been used to arrest pastors. Vigilante groups have forcibly re-converted Christians. Church attendance is discouraged in some educational settings.

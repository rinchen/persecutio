Yemen

Historical

After the Islamic conquests of the 7th century, Christianity in Yemen declined; the Himyarite kingdom had a brief Christian period in the 5th–6th centuries. Ottoman and British colonial contact renewed small Catholic and Protestant presences during the 19th century, which again contracted after mid-20th century conflicts.

Modern

Ongoing civil war involving Houthi, Saudi-led, and UAE-backed forces has devastated Yemen’s small Christian community. Houthi authorities impose strict religious controls. For Christians, humanitarian access and basic services can be restricted on the basis of religion or perceived loyalty. Proselytizing is banned under Houthi codes.

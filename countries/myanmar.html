Historical Background

Baptist, Catholic, and other missions expanded Christian education and health services in hill and border areas during the colonial period, with conversion later used to justify ethnic and political discrimination.

Modern-Day Situation

Both military rule and ethnic armed conflict expose Christians, especially Chin and Kachin communities, to village destruction, forced displacement, and conscription. Junta-era pressure intensified after the 2021 coup. As 2025 sees Myanmar entering a fifth year of an increasingly violent conflict, solutions - or even just a ceasefire - are nowhere in sight. After the army staged a coup on 1 February 2021, fighting has continued and increased in many parts of the country; with the levels of violence rising, the government resorts more and more to aerial and missile attacks. The 2008 constitution was drafted by a previous military regime and, according to the SAC, remains in force following the military coup.

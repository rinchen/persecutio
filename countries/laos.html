Laos

Historical Background

Catholic missionaries arrived in the 17th century through Portuguese and French channels; Protestant missions grew during French colonial rule (1893–1953). After 1975, the Lao People's Democratic Republic imposed strict controls, restricting religious practice to state-sanctioned outlets and driving churches underground.

Modern-Day Situation

The Lao government exercises strict control over all religious activity, restricting church construction, materials importation, and training of clergy. Protestant and Catholic converts face surveillance and local harassment; several Christians remain in prison. Decentralized village councils enforce local religious norms in many regions.

Historical Background

Christian missions operated among indigenous peoples in Sabah, Sarawak, and the peninsula from the 19th century onward. Modern pluralism coexists with Islamic legal frameworks limiting public Christian witness.

Sources: (2024) Open Doors World Watch List 2024

Modern-Day Situation

Christians face local restrictions on church construction, religious education, and proselytization while societal tensions sometimes turn violent on the Malay peninsula and in East Malaysian states. The constitution states, “Islam is the religion of the Federation; but other religions may be practiced in peace and harmony.” Federal and state governments have the power to mandate doctrine for Muslims and promote Sunni Islam above all other religious groups.

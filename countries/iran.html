Iran

Historical

Assyrian Christianity in Persia dates to the 1st century; Armenians have a continuous presence since the 4th century. After the 1979 Islamic Revolution, former Shah-era evangelistic institutions were closed; while “traditional” minorities retained some rights, apostasy charges rose in the 2000s.

Modern

Iran’s constitution recognizes Armenian and Assyrian Christian communities with reserved parliamentary representation, but converts from Islam face imprisonment, surveillance, and coerced “re-education.” House churches are raided; leaders receive lengthy sentences. Nationality laws restrict church-owned property transfers.

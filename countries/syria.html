Syria

Historical

Syria hosts some of Christianity’s oldest sites—Damascus, Antioch, Qamishli— and has native traditions of Syriac Orthodoxy dating to the 1st century. Under late Ottoman rule and French mandate (1920–1946), communal militias and intercommunal tensions intensified. Baathist secularism from the 1960s onward restricted religious expression and closed religious courts.

Modern

Years of civil war and occupation by ISIS (2014–2017) devastated Syria’s historic Christian communities in Homs, Qamishli, and along the Khabour valley. Many Assyrian, Armenian, and Greek Orthodox Christians have fled abroad. Territorially contiguous Islamist factions in the north impose Sharia-based restrictions on public life.

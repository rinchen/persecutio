Afghanistan

Historical Background

Catholicism was introduced by Spanish Jesuit missionaries in the early 17th century through Mughal court influence; later mission work was limited by geography and conflict. Christianity remained a marginal faith in Afghan society throughout the 20th century until the small indigenous community was suppressed first under Soviet occupation, then by the Taliban (1996–2001), and now again post-2021.

Modern-Day Situation

After the Taliban’s return to power (August 2021), reports indicate near- total suppression of non-Islamic worship. Any known Christian converts risk death or expulsion; house churches operate covertly. Bibles and Christian media are illegal. Aid agencies with religious affiliations are kept under strict surveillance.

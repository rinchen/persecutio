Historical Background

Christianity arrived through French colonization. The Christian minority remains small, concentrated in southern and western regions. Since 2015, jihadist violence from Nigeria and Mali has spread into Niger's border areas.

Sources: (2024) Open Doors World Watch List 2024

Modern-Day Situation

Islamic militant groups attack Christian communities and churches in border regions. Converts from Islam face severe family and community pressure. Proselytizing is legally restricted, and security conditions limit Christian activity in many areas. The jihadist group al-Sunnah wa Jama'ah (ASWJ) has made life increasingly difficult for Christians in Niger. In areas under their influence, Christian gatherings are severely restricted, and communal worship often occurs under the threat of violence.

Historical Background

Catholicism dominated Nicaraguan civic life from the 16th century through the 1979 revolution. The Sandinista period initially tolerated, then restricted, religious expression; after peace accords in the 1990s the church regained public stature before being again targeted in the 2020s.

Sources: (2024) Open Doors World Watch List 2024

Modern-Day Situation

Since 2018 and accelerating after 2021, the Ortega government has arrested, exiled, and criminalized opposition clergy. Catholic media and schools have been closed or seized, and bishops and priests face charges and house arrest. Religious institutions are under severe state scrutiny.

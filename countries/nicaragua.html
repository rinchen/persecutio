Nicaragua

Historical

Catholicism had an almost hegemonic role in Nicaragua from the 16th century until the 1979 Sandinista revolution, which initially permitted religious freedom but grew hostile after the radicalization of the 1980s. In the 1990s, the church regained public influence and mediated political conflict.

Modern

The Nicaraguan government under Daniel Ortega launched an unprecedented crackdown on the Catholic Church in 2018, arresting and exiling dozens of priests, closing Catholic media, and seizing church property. Dialogue between bishops and government collapsed; the apostolic nuncio was expelled. Opposition clergy face criminal charges or exile.

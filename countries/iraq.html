Historical Background

Mesopotamia is the cradle of Eastern Christianity, with communities dating to the 1st-2nd centuries. These communities thrived under the Abbasids, endured Timurid and Mongol raids, and continued under the Ottoman millet system, only to face existential threat in the 21st century from jihadist violence and mass displacement.

Sources: (2024) Open Doors World Watch List 2024

Modern-Day Situation

The 2014-2017 ISIS advance devastated Assyrian, Chaldean, and Armenian communities in Mosul and the Nineveh Plains. Many remain displaced. Returning Christians face militia pressure, land disputes, and fragile security. Church bombings and threats still recur.

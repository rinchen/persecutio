Iraq

Historical

Mesopotamia is the cradle of Eastern Christianity—Assyrian, Chaldean, and Syriac traditions stretch back to the 1st and 2nd centuries.Communities thrived through the Abbasid era, endured Mongol campaigns and Timurid raids, and continued under Ottoman millet system. The 20th century saw fluctuating fortunes under monarchy, republic, Baathism, and the post-2003 republic.

Modern

Since 2003 and especially after the ISIS caliphate (2014–2017), Iraq’s ancient Christian communities in Mosul, Nineveh Plains, and Baghdad have been decimated through targeted violence and forced displacement. Although ISIS territorial control ended, security remains fragile, and returning Christians face property disputes, discrimination, and militia pressure.

Eritrea

Historical

Christianity arrived in the 4th century through Syrian missionaries, making Eritrea one of Africa's oldest Christian regions. The Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church dominated for centuries; Latin Catholic presence grew after Italian colonization (1889–1941), and Protestant missions grew under British administration after World War II.

Modern

Eritrea extends tight state control over all recognized religious bodies— Orthodox, Catholic, Lutheran, and Sunni Islam. Unregistered evangelical churches were effectively banned in 2002; hundreds of unregistered Christians remain imprisoned without charge. Children are routinely interrogated about home worship.

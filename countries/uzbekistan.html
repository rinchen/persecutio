Uzbekistan

Historical Background

Christianity arrived in Transoxiana through Byzantine, Persian, and later Russian influences. The Soviet period suppressed churches and mosques alike; independence in 1991 restored formal religious practice but retained state controls that hardened after the 2005 Andijan protests.

Modern-Day Situation

Uzbekistan has gradually opened religious space since the late 2010s, yet restrictions on proselytizing, importation and printing of religious literature, and unregistered religious activity persist. Ethnic Uzbek and Russian converts to Christianity are occasionally fined or detained.

Colombia

Historical Background

Christianity was introduced through 16th-century missionaries working under the Spanish colonial system; Catholicism shaped civic life through haciendas, town festivals, and colonial institutions. Political violence between Liberals and Conservatives in the 19th and 20th centuries periodically targeted churches and their leadership.

Modern-Day Situation

In Colombia’s conflict-heavy zones, guerrillas, paramilitaries, and criminal gangs have targeted Catholic clergy, Protestant pastors, and church leaders who defend human rights, oppose illegal economies, or mediate with communities. Killings and threats declined after the 2016 peace agreement but continue in Caquetá, Nariño, and Norte de Santander.

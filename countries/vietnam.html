Vietnam

Historical

Catholicism arrived with Portuguese and French missionaries from the 16th century, proliferating during French colonial rule (1858–1954) especially in the north. The Second Vatican Council energized Vietnamese Catholic leadership; the post-1975 communist government restricted foreign religious influence, sending clergy to re-education camps.

Modern

Vietnam’s constitution guarantees religious freedom, but authorities require registration of religious organizations. Unregistered groups, particularly house churches and ethnic-minority Protestants in the Central Highlands and Northwest, face intermittent confiscation of property, harassment, detention, and pressure to renounce faith.

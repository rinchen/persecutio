North Korea

Historical Background

Before 1945, Pyongyang was a center of Christian activity in East Asia, earning the nickname “Jerusalem of the East.” After the Korean War and the consolidation of the Kim regime, North Korea became officially atheist in materialist outlook. From the 1950s onward, clergy and lay leaders were purged, churches were closed, and religious practice was driven underground.

Modern-Day Situation

The Democratic People's Republic of Korea is widely considered the most dangerous place in the world to be a Christian. Possession of a Bible, gathering for worship, or displaying symbols of the faith can result in imprisonment in kwan-liso (political prison camps). Defectors and underground networks report systematic elimination of religious practice. State ideology—Juche—functions as an enforced quasi-religion.
